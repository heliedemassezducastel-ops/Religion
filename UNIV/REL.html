7 Power Scriptures

A Journey of Belief & Light

Hi! My name is Hélie Demassez-du Castel, and I am a recent convert to The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. For my creative Discipleship project, I decided to create a small webpage using 7 scriptures that influenced me to join the Church and believe in the gospel.


I discovered the Church during my foreign exchange year in Draper, Utah, where I attended seminary and went to church every week with my host family. I then had lessons with the missionaries over the past three years and was finally baptized in November 2025.

Moroni 8:8-9

"Listen to the words of Christ, and hearken unto them and obey them; for behold the voice of the Lord came unto me by the power of the Holy Ghost, saying: Listen to the words of Christ, your Redeemer, your Lord and your God. Behold, I came into the world not to call the righteous but sinners to repentance; the whole need no physician, but they that are sick; wherefore, little children are whole, for they are not capable of committing sin; wherefore the curse of Adam is taken from them in me, that it hath no power over them; and the law of circumcision is done away in me. And after this manner did the Holy Ghost manifest the word of God unto me; wherefore, my beloved son, I know that it is solemn mockery before God, that ye should baptize little children."

Answers to Questions

When I was first introduced to the gospel, I had many questions that my Catholic beliefs could not answer. One of them was: “What happens to a baby who dies during pregnancy?” In the Catholic Church, I was taught that the baby would not go to heaven, and I felt that this was really unfair.

I then asked my seminary teacher for guidance and whether the Book of Mormon could answer my question. He calmly opened to Moroni 8:8 and explained what I needed to know. This was my first experience with the Book of Mormon, where I found answers that I could not find in the Bible, and it made my interest in the religion grow.

Alma 34:28-29

"And now behold, my beloved brethren, I say unto you, do not suppose that this is all; for after ye have done all these things, if ye turn away the needy, and the naked, and visit not the sick and afflicted, and impart of your substance, if ye have, to those who stand in need—I say unto you, if ye do not any of these things, behold, your prayer is vain, and availeth you nothing, and ye are as hypocrites who do deny the faith. Therefore, if ye do not remember to be charitable, ye are as dross, which the refiners do cast out, (it being of no worth) and is trodden under foot of men."

Applying Charity

The first time I actually paid attention to that verse, I was so astonished by how powerful it was. Alma really helped me understand how to become a better disciple and apply it to real life. I had a discussion at church about charity, and this verse was the first verse of the Book of Mormon that I was able to cite without being broad, knowing exactly what it meant and how meaningful it is for all of us.

2 Nephi 29:7-8

"Know ye not that there are more nations than one? Know ye not that I, the Lord your God, have created all men, and that I remember those who are upon the isles of the sea; and that I rule in the heavens above and in the earth beneath; and I bring forth my word unto the children of men, yea, even upon all the nations of the earth? Wherefore murmur ye, because that ye shall receive more of my word? Know ye not that the testimony of two nations is a witness unto you that I am God, that I remember one nation like unto another? Wherefore, I speak the same words unto one nation like unto another. And when the two nations shall run together the testimony of the two nations shall run together also."

The Truth Everywhere

What I have always loved about this verse is how open-minded it is. In my family, we all struggled with the idea of one church being true and the other one wrong. This verse totally built my testimony of the Church as it recognizes other churches and helps find truth everywhere. The Church is true because it’s the restored Gospel of Jesus Christ. Saying that this Church is true doesn’t imply that others are totally wrong, but we are the 'true' Church because the Priesthood has been restored.

Alma 32:28

"Now, we will compare the word unto a seed. Now, if ye give place, that a seed may be planted in your heart, behold, if it be a true seed, or a good seed, if ye do not cast it out by your unbelief, that ye will resist the Spirit of the Lord, behold, it will begin to swell within your breasts; and when you feel these swelling motions, ye will begin to say within yourselves—It must needs be that this is a good seed, or that the word is good, for it beginneth to enlarge my soul; yea, it beginneth to enlighten my understanding, yea, it beginneth to be delicious to me."

The Growing Seed

When I think of the work of the Lord inside of me, I think of this seed that He’s planted inside my heart. Every time I am able to serve others or to love one another better, I think of this seed growing and slowly becoming a tree. Later, this tree can produce fruits that I can give to others by being a good light and ministering unto them. This metaphor allows for so much interpretation, but I was truly able to build a testimony around it by applying it to my life and relating it to serving.

Ether 12:6

"And now, I, Moroni, would speak somewhat concerning these things; I would show unto the world that faith is things which are hoped for and not seen; wherefore, dispute not because ye see not, for ye receive no witness until after the trial of your faith."

Philosophy and Faith

In France, I remember learning a definition of faith during my philosophy class. This definition helped me feel the Spirit so much that I knew it was true. It stated: 'faith is to hope that something is real without having the proof of it.' When I first read this verse, I automatically thought of my philosophy teacher. He is not Christian and doesn’t have any knowledge of the Book of Mormon. His definition was simply built on the precepts he’s learned during his studies. I thought it was so powerful to know that the definition I learned in school was the same one I read in the Book of Mormon. It truly helped me understand the word 'faith' more, and it showed me that nothing happens without a purpose.

Enos 1:13

"And now behold, this was the desire which I desired of him—that if it should so be, that my people, the Nephites, should fall into transgression, and by any means the Lamanites should not be destroyed, that the Lord God would preserve a record of my people, the Nephites; even if it so be by the power of his holy arm, that it might be brought forth at some future day unto the Lamanites, that, perhaps, they might be brought unto salvation—"

The Power of Prayer

This chapter has fostered my testimony because the missionaries would always use it in their lessons. It is a one-chapter book, and that makes it really easy to read and to come back to when other books, such as the Book of Alma, are getting longer. What made this chapter so special to me is how powerful Enos’s prayer is. It is so amazing that he prayed for people to join him instead of only praying for the Nephites. He prayed for everyone. It showed me from the very beginning how loving and caring the Church is towards visitors and all non-members.

Moroni 7:45

"And charity suffereth long, and is kind, and envieth not, and is not puffed up, seeketh not her own, is not easily provoked, thinketh no evil, and rejoiceth not in iniquity but rejoiceth in the truth, beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things."

Attributes of Charity

This chapter has taught me so much about charity. In my life, I would always find refuge in the Bible to find some guidance about how to love others better, and finding this passage in the Book of Mormon was so helpful. It really taught me a lot about charity, and I can see all of the attributes stated in this verse apply today. I really found a testimony of this verse when it says 'charity suffereth long,' because sometimes I thought that love would never make me sad, whereas it’s normal sometimes to feel discouraged or disappointed in order for our love to increase.
